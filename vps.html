Frequently asked questions ...



What are VPS?

VPS are Linux-based virtual machines (VMs) that run on top of virtualized hardware. Each VPS you create is a new server you can use, either standalone or as part of a larger, cloud-based infrastructure.



Can I resize my VPS?

Yes. Resizing a VPS, also known as vertical scaling, increases the amount of CPU and RAM available to a VPS and permanently increases the size of a VPS’s disk.



Will I be billed if I power off my VPS?

Yes, when you power off your VPS, you are still billed for it. This is because your disk space, CPU, RAM, and IP address are all reserved, even while powered off. Therefore, charges are made until you destroy the instance.

This is For advanced developers who know how to manage a server or want to learn by their own. This is currently not on offer for business and enterprises.
